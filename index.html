Ada Lovelace

English mathematitician, writer, and considered the first computer programmer

  • Born on December 10, 1815
  • Parents: Lord and Lady Byron
    • Began studying mathematics at a young age.
  • At age 12, wrote her first book Flyology examining the dynamics of flight.
  • 1833: Introduced to Charles Babbage (father of computers) and the Difference Engine - a machine capable of performing simple calculations.
  • 1841: began translating a lecture of Babbage's about the Analyitcal Engine, based on the Difference Engine.
  • 1843: finished the translation with detailed notes that credit her as being the first computer programmer.
  • 1850: failed attempt to create a gambling machine to predict odds for horse races.
  • 1851: diagnosed with uterine cancer
  • Died November 27, 1852
